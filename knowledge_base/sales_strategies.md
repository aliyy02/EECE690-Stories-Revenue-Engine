## Cross-Sell & Upsell Tactics

### Complementary Pairing
- If customer orders coffee, suggest food item with highest co-occurrence at that branch
- Morning: pastry/croissant with hot coffee
- Afternoon: cookie/donut with cold drink
- Key metric: **Food Attachment Rate** = food qty / beverage qty per branch

### Modifier Upsell
- "Would you like oat milk?" / "Add an extra shot?" / "Make it large?"
- Low cost to business, high perceived value to customer
- Modifiers typically have 70%+ margins (nearly free revenue)
- Key metric: **Modifier Attach Rate** = modifier qty / total beverage qty per branch
- Staff scripts should be specific: "Would you like to try our oat milk upgrade for just [price]?"

### Size Upsell
- Customers default to Small - staff should suggest Medium or Large
- Margin per unit increases with size (ingredients scale sub-linearly)
- Key metric: **Size Distribution** = % Small vs Medium vs Large orders per product
- Script: "Would you like a Medium today? It's only [price diff] more"

### Time-Based Suggestions
- Morning rush: quick add-ons (pastry, extra shot)
- Afternoon lull: premium drinks, food combos
- Weekend: indulgent items (frappuccinos, specialty donuts)

## Menu Engineering (BCG Matrix for F&B)

### Stars (High Volume + High Margin)
- PROTECT: Never discount, never change recipe
- PROMOTE: Feature on menu boards, staff favorites, social media
- POSITION: Eye-level on menu, first items listed in category

### Plow Horses (High Volume + Low Margin)
- REPRICE: Slight increase (customers already love it, demand is inelastic)
- REDUCE COST: Find cheaper ingredients without quality loss
- PAIR: Bundle with high-margin item to lift overall ticket margin

### Puzzles (Low Volume + High Margin)
- PROMOTE: Better menu placement, staff recommendation scripts
- MARKET: Feature on social media, "hidden gem" positioning
- SAMPLE: Offer free tastings to build awareness
- These are the BIGGEST opportunity - high margin means every new sale is very profitable

### Dogs (Low Volume + Low Margin)
- KILL: Remove from menu unless they serve a strategic purpose
- Benefits of removal: less inventory, simpler training, faster service, reduced decision fatigue
- Exception: Keep if it fills a dietary need (vegan, gluten-free) that retains customers

## Pricing Tactics

### Price Anchoring
- Place highest-price item first in each category
- Makes everything else look reasonable by comparison
- Example: Put a $12 specialty drink at top, $6 latte feels like a deal

### Decoy Pricing (Size Strategy)
- Small: $4, Medium: $5.50, Large: $6
- Medium is the "decoy" - it makes Large look like the best value
- Most customers will choose Large (highest margin)

### Bundle Discount
- "Save [X] when you buy together"
- Increases AOV (average order value) even at slightly lower margin per item
- Bundle should be: top-volume beverage + top-margin food item
- Price at 5-10% below sum of individual prices

## Offer Stacking (Ecom Playbook Applied to Coffee)

### The Stack
1. **Core offer**: The coffee they came for
2. **Bump offer**: Modifier upsell at POS ("add oat milk?")
3. **Cross-sell**: Food suggestion ("goes great with our pistachio donut")
4. **The combo**: Pre-built bundle on menu board at slight discount

### Key Principle
- Each layer should feel natural, not pushy
- Staff training is critical: tone matters more than script
- Track conversion rate at each layer to optimize

## Operational Revenue Levers

### Reduce Waste = Increase Profit
- Forecast demand per branch per day using historical patterns
- Over-ordering perishables = waste = lost profit
- Under-ordering = stockouts = lost sales
- Both hurt: forecasting accuracy directly impacts bottom line

### Staff Efficiency
- Right number of staff at right times = faster service = more throughput = more revenue
- Understaffed during peak = long lines = lost customers
- Overstaffed during trough = labor cost eating margin

### Delivery Channel Expansion
- Each new delivery channel (Toters, etc.) = new revenue stream at marginal cost
- Delivery customers often order more per transaction (no "quick coffee" behavior)
- Key metric: delivery revenue as % of total, by branch

### New Branch Launch Strategy
- Some branches ramp 3x faster than others
- Identify what fast-ramp branches have in common (location type, marketing, menu mix)
- Replicate winning playbook for future openings

## Seasonal Strategy

### Seasonal Low Period (May-June)
- Revenue drops dramatically at most branches during this period
- Mall/Airport locations are more resilient
- Actions: reduce staff, reduce perishable inventory, launch value bundles
- This is a COST REDUCTION play, not a revenue growth play
- Note: This trough appears in May-June in the data, which may not align with Ramadan timing

### Summer Peak (Jul-Aug)
- Revenue peaks 2-3x over Ramadan trough
- Cold bar becomes dominant: Frappuccinos, Cold Brew, Blended Drinks
- Actions: hire seasonal staff, stock cold bar ingredients, push cold menu on social media
- This is your REVENUE MAXIMIZATION window

### Holiday Season (Nov-Dec)
- Moderate bump, especially at mall locations
- Seasonal menu items (holiday drinks) can drive excitement
- Gift card promotions increase customer acquisition
